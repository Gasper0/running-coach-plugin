# Session Library — Running Coach

Full catalogue of training sessions organized by type and phase.
Claude should select and adapt sessions from this library when building a plan.

## ENDURANCE FONDAMENTALE (EF)

### EF-30 — Easy 30 min
- Phase: Base
- Duration: 30 min | Distance: ~5km
- Zone: Z1 (65–72% VMA)
- Warmup: 5 min walk → jog
- Main: 25 min at EF pace (conversational)
- Cooldown: 3 min walk + stretching
- Notes: Perfect for recovery days or early base weeks

### EF-45 — Easy 45 min
- Phase: Base / Build
- Duration: 45 min | Distance: ~7–8km
- Zone: Z1
- Warmup: 10 min easy
- Main: 35 min at EF pace
- Cooldown: 5 min stretching
- Notes: Standard mid-week easy run

### EF-60 — Easy 60 min
- Phase: Build / Peak
- Duration: 60 min | Distance: ~9–10km
- Zone: Z1
- Warmup: 10 min
- Main: 50 min at EF pace. Option: last 10 min at Z2.
- Cooldown: 5 min
- Notes: Good volume builder without accumulated fatigue

### EF-STRIDES — Easy with Strides
- Phase: Base / Build
- Duration: 40 min | Distance: ~7km
- Warmup: 15 min EF → 6×80m strides (fast but controlled, not sprinting) with 90s walk recovery → 20 min EF → cooldown
- Notes: Neuromuscular activation. Strides improve running economy.

## SORTIE LONGUE (SL)

### SL-10 — Long Run 10km
- Phase: Base
- Duration: 60–70 min | Distance: 10km
- Zone: Z1–Z2
- Profile: Progressive. First half Z1, second half Z2.
- Notes: Entry-level long run for 10km plan

### SL-14 — Long Run 14km
- Phase: Build
- Duration: 80–90 min | Distance: 14km
- Zone: Z1 throughout
- Notes: Fuel at 45 min (gel or banana). Hydrate every 20 min.

### SL-16 — Long Run 16km
- Phase: Build / Peak (semi)
- Duration: 90–105 min | Distance: 16km
- Zone: Z1 (first 12km) → Z2 last 4km
- Notes: First long run requiring mid-run nutrition

### SL-18 — Long Run 18km
- Phase: Peak (semi)
- Duration: 100–115 min | Distance: 18km
- Zone: Z1–Z2
- Notes: Key confidence-builder for semi-marathon. Fuel at 45 min + 75 min.

### SL-20 — Long Run 20km
- Phase: Peak (semi, advanced)
- Duration: 110–125 min | Distance: 20km
- Zone: Z1 (first 14km) → option: last 6km at marathon pace Z2
- Notes: Longest run for semi-marathon. 2–3 weeks before race. Mandatory nutrition.

### SL-PROG — Progressive Long Run
- Phase: Peak
- Duration: 90 min | Distance: 15–18km
- Profile: First third Z1 → middle third Z2 → last third Z3
- Notes: Excellent race simulation. Teaches the runner to finish strong.

### SL-22 — Long Run 22km
- Phase: Build (marathon) / Peak (semi, advanced)
- Duration: 125–140 min | Distance: 22km
- Zone: Z1–Z2
- Notes: Fuel at 45 min + 90 min. Good stepping stone towards marathon-distance long runs.

### SL-26 — Long Run 26km
- Phase: Build (marathon)
- Duration: 150–170 min | Distance: 26km
- Zone: Z1 (first 20km) → Z2 last 6km
- Notes: Mandatory mid-run nutrition every 30–40 min after first hour. Take it easy — this is volume, not speed.

### SL-30 — Long Run 30km
- Phase: Peak (marathon)
- Duration: 175–200 min | Distance: 30km
- Zone: Z1 throughout. Option: last 8km at marathon pace Z2.
- Notes: Key confidence-builder for marathon. Fuel every 30 min (gels + electrolytes). Plan hydration stops. This is the longest training run — never go beyond 34km.

### SL-34 — Long Run 34km (advanced marathon only)
- Phase: Peak (marathon, advanced)
- Duration: 200–230 min | Distance: 34km
- Zone: Z1 throughout
- Notes: Only for experienced marathoners targeting < 3h30. Max 1 run at this distance during the plan. Requires full recovery (48–72h easy/rest after). Mandatory nutrition strategy rehearsal.

### SL-MARATHON-PACE — Marathon Pace Long Run
- Phase: Peak (marathon)
- Duration: 90–100 min | Distance: 16–18km
- Profile: 30 min Z1 warm-up → 45–60 min at marathon pace (Z2–Z3) → 10 min Z1 cooldown
- Notes: Race dress rehearsal. Wear race kit, use race nutrition. Practice fueling at pace. 3–4 weeks before race.

## HYROX SESSIONS (HX)

### HX-FUNC — Hyrox Functional Session
- Phase: Build / Peak
- Duration: 50–60 min
- Warmup: 10 min easy jog or row
- Main: Circuit of 4–6 Hyrox stations (choose from: ski erg 1000m, sled push 50m, sled pull 50m, burpee broad jumps 80m, rowing 1000m, farmers carry 200m, wall balls 75 reps, sandbag lunges 200m)
- Recovery: 2 min between stations
- Cooldown: 5 min stretching
- Notes: Moderate intensity. Focus on technique and transitions. Counts as a HARD session for running fatigue purposes.

### HX-SIM — Hyrox Full Simulation
- Phase: Peak (max once every 3–4 weeks)
- Duration: 75–90 min
- Profile: 8 × (1km run + 1 Hyrox station) — simulating race conditions at target race pace
- Notes: RACE EFFORT — requires 48–72h full recovery. Only schedule easy runs or rest the 2 days following. Wear race gear and practice transitions.

### Weekly template: Hyrox + Running (example: 3 running + 2 Hyrox)

| Day | Session | Fatigue |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Mon | Rest | — |
| Tue | Quality run (tempo or intervals) | HARD |
| Wed | HX-FUNC (functional) | HARD |
| Thu | Easy run (Z1, 40 min) | Easy |
| Fri | Rest or RA-MOBILITY | — |
| Sat | Long run | Moderate |
| Sun | HX-FUNC (lighter, technique focus) | Moderate |

**Rules**: never 2 HARD sessions back-to-back. Long run is protected (no hard Hyrox the day before). If HX-FUNC includes running intervals, it replaces the week's quality run — do NOT add another.

## FRACTIONNÉ (FR) — Intervals

### FR-400 — 400m Repeats (intro)
- Phase: Build
- Duration: 50 min | Distance: 9–10km total
- Warmup: 15 min Z1 + 4 strides
- Main: 8×400m at Z5 (95–100% VMA), 90s jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min Z1
- Notes: Start with 6 reps if new to intervals. All reps should feel equally hard.

### FR-400-ADV — 400m Repeats (advanced)
- Phase: Peak
- Duration: 60 min
- Warmup: 15 min + strides
- Main: 12×400m at Z5, 75s recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min

### FR-1000 — 1km Repeats
- Phase: Build / Peak
- Duration: 55–65 min | Distance: 12km total
- Warmup: 15 min Z1
- Main: 6×1000m at Z4–Z5 (race pace for 10km, or slightly faster), 2 min jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min Z1
- Notes: Core session for 10km preparation

### FR-1000-SEMI — 1km Repeats for Semi
- Phase: Build / Peak
- Duration: 65 min
- Warmup: 15 min
- Main: 8×1000m at Z4 (semi race pace), 90s jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min
- Notes: Builds confidence at target semi pace

### FR-2000 — 2km Repeats
- Phase: Peak
- Duration: 65–75 min
- Warmup: 15 min
- Main: 4×2000m at Z3–Z4, 3 min jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min
- Notes: Excellent threshold builder. Harder psychologically — focus on even splits.

### FR-PYRAMIDE — Pyramid Session
- Phase: Build
- Duration: 60 min
- Warmup: 15 min
- Main: 200m / 400m / 600m / 800m / 600m / 400m / 200m at Z5, recovery = half effort duration
- Cooldown: 10 min
- Notes: Varied session — good change of pace from standard repeats

### FR-COTE — Hill Repeats
- Phase: Base / Build
- Duration: 45 min
- Warmup: 15 min easy
- Main: 8×60s uphill at hard effort (Z4–Z5), walk back down as recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min easy
- Notes: Builds power and strength. Excellent injury prevention. Requires hill access.

## ALLURE SPÉCIFIQUE (AS) — Race Pace

### AS-TEMPO-20 — 20 min Tempo
- Phase: Build
- Duration: 45 min
- Warmup: 10 min Z1
- Main: 20 min continuous at Z3 (seuil / threshold)
- Cooldown: 10 min Z1
- Notes: Intro to sustained effort. Feel "comfortably hard" — can speak in short sentences.

### AS-TEMPO-30 — 30 min Tempo
- Phase: Build / Peak
- Duration: 50 min
- Warmup: 10 min
- Main: 30 min at Z3
- Cooldown: 10 min

### AS-10KM — 10km Race Pace Reps
- Phase: Peak
- Duration: 60 min
- Warmup: 15 min + strides
- Main: 3×2km at 10km race pace (Z4), 3 min jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min
- Notes: Calibrate race pace. Don't go faster — the goal is pace precision, not personal record.

### AS-SEMI — Semi-Marathon Race Pace Reps
- Phase: Peak
- Duration: 75 min
- Warmup: 15 min
- Main: 2×6km at semi-marathon pace (Z3–Z4), 5 min jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min
- Notes: Key session. This is what your race will feel like. Use GPS to lock pace.

### AS-NEGATIVE — Negative Split Run
- Phase: Peak
- Duration: 60–70 min | Distance: 12–14km
- Profile: First half at Z2, second half at Z3–Z4. Finish last 2km at race pace.
- Notes: Trains the runner to start conservatively and finish strong — the ideal race strategy.

## RENFORCEMENT MUSCULAIRE (RM)

### RM-BASIC — Basic Strength Circuit (30 min, no equipment)
- Sets: 3 rounds
- Exercises:
  - Squats: 15 reps
  - Fentes (alternating lunges): 12 reps/leg
  - Pont fessier (glute bridge): 15 reps
  - Gainage frontal (plank): 45 sec
  - Gainage latéral (side plank): 30 sec/side
  - Élévations de mollets (calf raises): 20 reps
  - Superman (back extensions): 12 reps
- Rest: 45s between exercises, 90s between rounds
- Notes: Do on non-running days or after easy runs. Core and glutes = injury prevention.

### RM-RUNNER — Runner-Specific Circuit (40 min)
- Sets: 3 rounds
- Exercises:
  - Single-leg squat (pistol assist): 8 reps/leg
  - Nordic curl or Romanian deadlift: 10 reps
  - Step-ups: 12 reps/leg
  - Lateral band walks: 15 steps/direction (with resistance band if available)
  - Copenhagen plank: 20 sec/side
  - Calf raises (single leg): 15 reps/leg
  - Dead bug: 10 reps/side
- Notes: Focuses on running-specific muscles. Reduces injury risk significantly.

### RM-CORE — Core Focus (20 min)
- 3 rounds:
  - Plank: 60 sec
  - Side plank: 40 sec/side
  - Russian twists: 20 reps
  - Hollow hold: 30 sec
  - Bird-dog: 12 reps/side
  - Mountain climbers: 20 reps
- Notes: Quick and effective. Good for busy weekday evenings.

## RÉCUPÉRATION ACTIVE (RA)

### RA-EASY — Easy Recovery Jog + Stretch (25 min)
- 10 min very easy jog or brisk walk (Z1 low, truly conversational)
- 15 min stretching:
  - Hip flexor lunge stretch: 60 sec/side
  - Pigeon pose or seated piriformis: 60 sec/side
  - Hamstring stretch (standing or seated): 45 sec/side
  - Quad stretch: 45 sec/side
  - Calf stretch (straight + bent knee): 45 sec/side
  - Glute stretch: 45 sec/side
- Notes: Do the day after a hard session. Promotes blood flow without adding fatigue.

### RA-MOBILITY — Mobility Flow (20 min, no running)
- Dynamic warm-up flow:
  - Leg swings (front/back + lateral): 10 reps/direction
  - Hip circles: 10 reps/direction
  - Ankle circles: 10 reps/direction
  - Thoracic rotations: 10 reps/side
  - World's greatest stretch: 5 reps/side
- Static finishers (hold 60 sec each): hip flexor, hamstring, piriformis, calf
- Notes: Excellent on total rest days. Takes only 20 min and significantly reduces injury risk.

## DELOAD / TAPER SESSIONS

### DL-EASY — Deload Easy Run (30–35 min)
- Pure EF, Z1, no intervals, no tempo
- Focus on form and relaxation
- Notes: During deload or taper weeks. Volume is reduced but quality maintained.

### TAPER-STRIDES — Pre-Race Activation
- 2 days before race
- 20 min easy jog → 4×100m strides at race pace → 10 min easy
- Total: 30 min
- Notes: Keeps legs fresh and sharp. Do not skip this — legs need activation before race day.
