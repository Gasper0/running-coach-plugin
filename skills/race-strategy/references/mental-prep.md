# Mental preparation — personalized course guide

## Principle

The mental guide is delivered as a separate text response, AFTER the visual widget. It must be:

1. **Personalized to the analyzed course** — not generic advice. Reference specific km numbers and physical features identified in the elevation profile.
2. **Concrete and usable** — give the runner specific phrases they can say to themselves during the race.
3. **Structured by race moments** — start, mid-race critical moments, final push.

## Output format

Write 3–5 short paragraphs in prose (not bullets). Each paragraph follows the same internal structure:

> **[Where: km X / specific feature]** — [What happens physiologically and psychologically] — [Mental cue: a concrete phrase the runner can recall].

Example for a 10km with climb at km 5–7:

> **Km 1 — le moment du départ.** L'adrénaline et la foule vont te pousser à partir 10 sec/km trop vite. Tu vas avoir l'impression de courir au ralenti par rapport à ceux qui te dépassent. C'est exactement ce qu'il faut. Ta phrase mentale : "le premier kilomètre n'est jamais celui qui gagne la course, c'est celui qui la perd". Laisse-les partir.

## Required moments to cover

For every race, include paragraphs covering:

### A — Race start (always)
The first km is the most common pacing mistake. Address it explicitly with reference to the conservative split prescribed in the strategy.

### B — Mid-race critical moments (course-specific)
Identify the 1–2 hardest physical moments on this specific course based on the profile:
- A climb? Address how to manage effort vs pace.
- A long flat section? Address mental fatigue and how to keep focus.
- A technical section? Address awareness vs flow.

If the course has a unique feature (a tunnel, a bridge, a signature landmark), reference it as a mental anchor.

### C — The "wall zone" (distance-specific)
- 5km: rarely needed
- 10km: km 7–8 mental dip — when "it's getting long but not over yet"
- Half: km 14–17 — physiological wall
- Marathon: km 30–35 — the real wall, glycogen depletion

Address this even if the profile is flat — it's a physiological reality.

### D — The final push (always)
Address the last 1–2 km (10km/half) or last 5km (marathon). The runner needs permission to suffer here, and a clear mental cue to commit.

## Mental cue patterns

These are tested phrases that work well in race context. Adapt them to the specific moment.

### For pace control
- "Je maintiens l'effort, pas l'allure."
- "La descente est pour bientôt."
- "Le premier km n'est jamais celui qui gagne la course, c'est celui qui la perd."

### For climbs
- "Cette montée, je la respecte. Je la rembourse en descente."
- "Mes concurrents souffrent autant que moi sur cette bosse."
- "Effort constant, foulée courte, je raccourcis le geste."

### For fatigue dips
- "Cette sensation, je la connais — elle passe en 90 secondes."
- "Plus que [X] km. C'est rien."
- "Si je me sens mal maintenant, c'est que je suis à la bonne intensité."

### For the final push
- "Je n'ai plus à économiser. Tout ce qui reste va dans l'arrivée."
- "Je veux pouvoir me dire que j'ai tout donné."
- "Ce dernier km, c'est celui dont je me souviendrai."

## What to avoid

- **Do NOT** write generic motivational fluff ("believe in yourself", "you've got this"). Always tie advice to a specific physical or tactical moment.
- **Do NOT** make the mental guide longer than the strategy widget — 3–5 paragraphs is the right length.
- **Do NOT** use bullet points or lists in the mental prep section — prose is more memorable in race context.
- **Do NOT** repeat what's already in the tactical notes of the widget. The mental guide is the **why** and the **how to think about it**, not the **what**.

## Distance-specific anchors

### 5km
- Whole race is one long sprint after km 1. Mental focus is on holding form when lactate burns.
- Mental cue zone: km 3–4 ("the burn").

### 10km
- Two halves: build the rhythm (km 1–5), commit to it (km 6–10).
- Mental cue zone: km 7–8 ("not over yet, but close enough to push").

### Half-marathon
- Three thirds: discover (km 1–7), execute (km 8–14), commit (km 15–21).
- Mental cue zone: km 14–17 (the wall) and km 19–21 (the finish).

### Marathon
- Four quarters: settle (km 1–10), execute (km 10–25), survive (km 25–35), finish (km 35–42).
- Mental cue zone: km 30–35 (real wall) and km 38–42 (commit zone).
