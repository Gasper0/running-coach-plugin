# Nutrition Protocols — Detailed by Distance

> Detailed nutrition reference for race-week, adapted to the demands of each distance.
> Macros are estimates per kg of bodyweight. The user adjusts to feel.

---

## General principles (all distances)

- **Glucides (carbs)** are the priority macro. The longer the race, the more glycogen needed.
- **Protéines (protein)** stay constant (1.4-1.6 g/kg) or rise for muscular events (Hyrox).
- **Lipides (fat)** decrease as race day approaches — slow digestion, less needed.
- **Fibres (fiber)** decrease sharply J-2 and J-1 to reduce GI volume on race day.
- **Hydratation** rises gradually J-3 onward.

---

## 10 km

A 10km uses both aerobic and anaerobic systems but rarely depletes glycogen stores. No need to "load" — eat normally with a slight carb emphasis on J-1.

### Macros target

| Day | Carbs | Protein | Fat | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| J-3 | 5-6 g/kg | 1.4-1.6 g/kg | 1 g/kg | Normal day |
| J-2 | 6-7 g/kg | 1.4-1.6 g/kg | 0.8 g/kg | Slight ↑ carbs |
| J-1 | 7-8 g/kg | 1.4-1.6 g/kg | 0.7 g/kg | Pasta dinner, easy on fats |
| J0 morning | 60-80g (single meal) | 5-10g | minimal | 3h before start |

### Race-morning breakfast example (60-80g carbs)

- 100g porridge with banana and honey + 250ml almond milk = ~70g carbs
- OR 2-3 slices white bread with jam + small yogurt = ~65g carbs
- Coffee if habitual (no new caffeine doses)

### During the race

For a sub-50min 10km, no fueling needed. Just water at the aid stations if hot.
For a longer 10km (>50min), an isotonic drink or one gel halfway can help.

## Half-marathon (21 km)

A half-marathon depletes most of liver glycogen and dips into muscle glycogen. Modest loading helps.

### Macros target

| Day | Carbs | Protein | Fat | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| J-4 | 6 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 1 g/kg | Normal |
| J-3 | 7 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.9 g/kg | ↑ Carbs, normal training |
| J-2 | 8 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.8 g/kg | Loading begins |
| J-1 | 8-9 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.7 g/kg | Loading peak, low fiber |
| J0 morning | 80-100g | 10-15g | minimal | 3h before start |

### Race-morning breakfast example (80-100g carbs)

- 150g porridge + banana + honey + jam = ~90g carbs + 12g protein
- OR 3 slices toast + jam + honey + small fruit = ~85g carbs
- Coffee if habitual

### During the race

Plan 1-2 gels (30g carbs each) at km 8 and km 14-16. Water at aid stations.

### J-1 dinner example

- 200g cooked pasta + tomato sauce + grilled chicken (100g) + olive oil drizzle
- White bread / baguette on the side
- No salad, no raw vegetables
- 1 dessert (rice pudding, banana cake)
- ~140g carbs, 35g protein, low fiber

## Marathon (42 km)

A marathon empties glycogen stores almost entirely. Full carb loading from J-3 is standard practice and supported by strong evidence.

### Macros target

| Day | Carbs | Protein | Fat | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| J-5 | 6-7 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 1 g/kg | Volume reduction starts |
| J-4 | 7-8 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.9 g/kg | Pre-load |
| J-3 | 10 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.7 g/kg | Full loading begins |
| J-2 | 10-12 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.6 g/kg | Loading peak |
| J-1 | 10-12 g/kg | 1.5 g/kg | 0.5 g/kg | Low fiber, low fat |
| J0 morning | 100-130g | 10-15g | minimal | 3h before start |

### Race-morning breakfast example (100-130g carbs)

- 200g porridge + banana + honey + jam + drizzle of maple syrup = ~120g carbs
- + small glass of fruit juice = ~10g carbs
- Coffee if habitual
- No yogurt, no nuts, nothing high-fiber

### During the race

Plan 5-7 gels for a 3h-4h marathon (one every 25-30 min from km 7-8 onward). 60-90g carbs/hour is the target for trained marathoners.

### J-1 dinner — Marathon-specific

- 250-300g cooked pasta + low-fat tomato sauce + small portion of grilled chicken
- 2-3 slices white bread
- Banana cake or rice pudding for dessert
- ~180g carbs, 35g protein, very low fiber/fat
- Avoid: raw vegetables, salad, beans, whole grains, cheese sauces

## Hyrox (Solo, Doubles, Pro)

Hyrox is unique: 8 km of running broken by 8 strength stations. The body uses both aerobic (running) and muscular (stations) systems. Protein matters more than for pure running events.

### Macros target

| Day | Carbs | Protein | Fat | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| J-3 | 6-7 g/kg | **1.8-2.0 g/kg** | 0.9 g/kg | Higher protein than running events |
| J-2 | 7-8 g/kg | **1.8-2.0 g/kg** | 0.8 g/kg | Loading begins |
| J-1 | 8 g/kg | **1.8 g/kg** | 0.7 g/kg | Moderate loading + protein |
| J0 morning | 80-100g | 20-25g | minimal | 2.5-3h before start |

### Race-morning breakfast example (Hyrox)

- 150g porridge + banana + honey + 30g whey protein + jam = ~90g carbs + 30g protein
- OR 3 toasts + scrambled egg whites + jam = ~80g carbs + 22g protein
- Coffee if habitual

### During the race

Hyrox typically lasts 60-90 minutes. One gel at the midway point (around station 4) is usually enough. Water at the aid points.

### Hyrox-specific J-1 considerations

- **Avoid heavy fiber the day before** — sled work and wall balls under digestive load is uncomfortable
- **Stay hydrated with electrolytes** — sodium loss is high during station work
- **Light protein at dinner** — chicken breast, white fish, eggs — not red meat

### J-1 dinner example (Hyrox)

- 200g cooked pasta + tomato sauce + 120g grilled chicken
- White bread on the side
- ~140g carbs, 40g protein
- Skip the dessert if anxious about digestion; small fruit is OK

## Hydration protocols

### Daily targets

| Day | Plain water | Add electrolytes? |
|---|---|---|
| J-7 to J-4 | 30-35 ml/kg | No (unless hot weather) |
| J-3 | 35-40 ml/kg | Optional |
| J-2 | 35-40 ml/kg | Yes, light dose |
| J-1 | 40 ml/kg | Yes, especially evening (500-700mg sodium) |
| J0 morning | 400-500ml in 2 hours | Yes, light dose |

### Signs of good hydration

- Urine: pale yellow (not transparent — overhydration)
- Body weight stable (don't lose more than 1% J-1 to J0)
- No headache, no fatigue from dehydration

### Race-morning hydration

- Wake-up: small glass of water (200ml)
- Breakfast: 300-400ml with breakfast
- 1h before start: sip 200ml
- 30 min before: stop drinking large amounts (avoid bathroom emergency)
- Last sip: 100ml in the final 15 min before start

---

## Special situations

### Hot weather (forecast > 25°C on race day)

- Increase J-2 and J-1 hydration to 45 ml/kg
- J-1 evening: add electrolytes systematically
- Race morning: pre-cool with cold drink, consider a cap soaked in cold water

### Cold weather (forecast < 5°C)

- Same nutrition, slightly less hydration (35 ml/kg) — sweat losses are lower
- Race morning: warm drink at breakfast helps body temperature
- Don't skip electrolytes — cold air still triggers sweating during effort

### Travel for race (different city/country)

- Day of travel: avoid heavy meals on transport
- Bring familiar snacks (banana cake, energy bars, oats)
- Arrive 1-2 days early if possible to adapt
- Test water locally — bottled water is safe everywhere

### GI sensitivity history

If the user has known GI issues during racing:
- Reduce fiber from J-3 (not just J-1)
- Avoid lactose if relevant — switch to almond/oat milk
- Pre-race meal: simple white toast + jam, nothing fancy
- During race: only gels and products tested in training
